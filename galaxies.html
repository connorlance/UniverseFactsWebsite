    • Number of Stars:

    There are 250 billion stars in the Milky Way galaxy, without about half of those having planets. There are between 100 to 200 billion galaxies in the observable universe. Multiply those together and the number of estimated stars in the observable universe is between 125 followed by 22 zeros to 250 followed by 22 zeros.
